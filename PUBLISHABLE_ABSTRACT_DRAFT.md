# Publishable abstract draft

We present a native-free, self-deciding contact-prediction pipeline designed to separate true structure signal from weak or noisy evolutionary evidence without relying on AlphaFold as a hidden oracle. The system combines DCA-derived anchors, sequence-only physical priors, secondary-structure-like local priors, degree consistency, event-region closure evidence, and iterative distance-geometry diffusion. On the difficult 4AKE adenylate kinase benchmark, the full no-AlphaFold diffusion pathway converged without runtime hangs or visual-artifact generation, improved recall compared with the previous physics-only baseline, and then correctly abstained from a solved claim. The best native-free mode achieved 0.1818 contact precision and 0.1431 contact recall, below the predeclared 0.70/0.70 solved threshold. The result shows that hand-coded physics plus geometric diffusion can amplify weak signal but cannot reliably resolve 4AKE when the initial anchor field is sparse and noisy. The framework therefore acts as an honest benchmarkable system: it reports success when independent evidence is sufficient and explicitly rejects overclaiming when the available information is inadequate. A real MSA-free learned structure prior, such as a non-AlphaFold ESMFold/OmegaFold/SPIRED predicted PDB, remains the next required independent evidence source for a final 4AKE no-AlphaFold test.
